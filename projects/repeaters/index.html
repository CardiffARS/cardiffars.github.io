Repeaters


Repeaters provide a convenient way for low power portable radios to be used to talk over a wide area by, unsuprisingly, repeating the signals. In addition to increasing the range across which people can talk, they also provide a meeting place. Cardiff is covered by several repeaters.

Analog

These repeaters use narrow band FM, the most common standard for hand held radios.

GB3BC

Repeater Information

Input Frequency: 145.1500 MHz
Output Frequency: 145.7500 MHz
CTCSS: 94.8 Hz

GB3WB

Repeater Information

Input Frequency 434.900 MHz Output Frequency: 433.300 MHz CTCSS: 94.8 Hz

Digital

These repeaters use DMR, D-STAR, or System Fusion modulation. While less common these digital modes have advantages in sound quality in cases when the signal is a little weaker. Unfortunately radios supporting these modes are usually more expensive than NBFM ones.
